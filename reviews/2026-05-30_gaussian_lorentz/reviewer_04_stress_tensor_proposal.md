## Findings Ordered By Severity

1. **Critical: the real-space stress-tensor density layer is absent, and the roadmap drift hides the absence.** CA-23 says CA-27 will handle "real-space densities and coefficient formulas" and CA-28 will handle residuals (`report/sections/23_gaussian_boson_lorentz_roadmap.tex:90`--`99`). The actual CA-27 is residual conditions and CA-28 is the numerical suite (`report/README.md:46`--`48`; `report/sections/27_gaussian_boson_residual_conditions.tex:1`--`6`; `report/sections/28_gaussian_boson_numerical_suite.tex:1`--`6`). This matters because the stress-energy proposal needs local candidates for `T_{00}`, `T_{01}`, and `T_{11}`, while the current Gaussian block only fixes the total scalar Hamiltonian and one-particle symbols (`report/sections/24_gaussian_boson_symbol_calculus.tex:35`--`49`; `report/sections/26_gaussian_boson_generator_algebra.tex:43`--`52`).

2. **High: the one-particle momentum `P_a = k_a` is not connected to the real-space lattice current generated by the energy first moment.** CA-12 derives `i[H,K_x] = sum_j (x_{j+1}-x_j)i[h_j,h_{j+1}]` for an open nearest-neighbour density decomposition (`report/sections/12_lattice_boost_current_1d.tex:66`--`73`) and interprets the unit-spacing sum as a bulk momentum candidate (`report/sections/12_lattice_boost_current_1d.tex:75`--`97`). CA-26 instead defines `P_a^{(1)}=k_a` and `K_a^{(1)}=1/2(X_a h+hX_a)` on a smooth momentum core (`report/sections/26_gaussian_boson_generator_algebra.tex:27`--`54`) and then second-quantizes (`report/sections/26_gaussian_boson_generator_algebra.tex:154`--`170`). There is no checked bridge showing that the integrated lattice energy current from a chosen Gaussian density converges to, or equals at finite scale, `dGamma(k_a)`.

3. **High: identifying energy flux with momentum density is still a proposal, not a checked lattice stress-tensor statement.** The continuum 1+1 CFT source has a symmetric energy-momentum tensor and the conservation equation `partial_0 T_{mu0}+partial_1 T_{mu1}=0` (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:5046`--`5052`). CA-12 only proves a finite commutator identity from a Hamiltonian-density ansatz (`report/sections/12_lattice_boost_current_1d.tex:13`--`22`, `:99`--`:104`). Therefore `i[h_j,h_{j+1}]` is a good candidate for an energy-current/momentum-density layer, but the equality `T_{01}=T_{10}` and the existence of a full local `T_{\mu\nu}^{lat}` need separate source, convention, and convergence evidence.

4. **High: stress-tensor normalizations are not fixed where the report starts using stress-tensor intuition.** `CONVENTIONS.md` still marks CFT normalizations as not fixed (`CONVENTIONS.md:86`--`91`) and lattice conventions as not fixed (`CONVENTIONS.md:93`--`98`). CA-14 cites a source where the Hamiltonian density is built from chiral and anti-chiral energy-momentum tensors (`references/text/CFTFromLatticeFermions.txt:2133`--`2139`) and where density modes are linear combinations of Virasoro generators (`references/text/CFTFromLatticeFermions.txt:2163`--`2169`). Before a proposal shard writes formulas for `T_{00}`, `T_{01}`, `T_{11}`, it must pin the sign, metric, `2pi`, circle-length, and chiral/full conventions.

5. **Medium-high: the Gaussian Hamiltonian-density split is not a convention yet.** The total scalar tier is fixed as `H = 1/2 sum_x p_x^2 + 1/2 sum_{x,r} q_x V_r q_{x+r}` (`CONVENTIONS.md:194`--`205`; `report/sections/24_gaussian_boson_symbol_calculus.tex:35`--`49`), sourced by the free lattice Hamiltonian and dispersion (`literature/md/2010.11121/2010.11121.md:598`--`603`, `:614`--`:623`). But a boost/stress proposal needs a local `h_x`, and CA-23 explicitly warns that `h_x -> h_x + div b_x` changes first moments by boundary/current terms (`report/sections/23_gaussian_boson_lorentz_roadmap.tex:114`--`119`). A site-anchored split and a symmetric bond/cell split are different lattice stress-tensor candidates until a convention fixes one.

6. **Medium: periodic finite tests cannot yet test first-moment stress-tensor candidates.** CA-17 states that `x_j=j` is not single-valued on a periodic chain and recommends a branch, sawtooth, Fourier modes, or another construction (`report/sections/17_lattice_symmetry_examples_queue.tex:61`--`68`). CA-23 repeats that early finite tests should use momentum-space generators or open-chain checks until a branch is fixed (`report/sections/23_gaussian_boson_lorentz_roadmap.tex:121`--`124`). CA-28's next numerical milestone explicitly defers open-boundary first-moment comparisons until a boundary convention exists (`report/sections/28_gaussian_boson_numerical_suite.tex:110`--`121`).

7. **Medium: the anisotropic and doubler counterexamples are useful, but they are only coefficient-level rejection witnesses.** CA-27 correctly rejects anisotropic cones by the Hessian residual (`report/sections/27_gaussian_boson_residual_conditions.tex:98`--`115`) and rejects the doubler symbol as a single-patch scalar target (`report/sections/27_gaussian_boson_residual_conditions.tex:117`--`131`), with tests at `test/runtests.jl:187`--`205` and `test/runtests.jl:220`--`221`. A stress-tensor proposal still needs to check whether the candidate local currents and stress components expose the same failures, not only whether `omega(k)^2` has bad Taylor or patch data.

8. **Medium: unbounded-generator and common-core issues remain explicitly open.** CA-26 says its commutators are identities on a smooth core and that promotion to self-adjoint generator statements is separate (`report/sections/26_gaussian_boson_generator_algebra.tex:53`--`54`, `:150`--`:152`). CA-15 requires a topology, state sequence, renormalisation maps, dense domain/core, and observable covariance before a continuum symmetry claim (`report/sections/15_lattice_symmetry_acceptance_tests.tex:54`--`84`). A stress-energy density proposal should not be phrased as a continuum theorem until those CA-15 items are attached.

## Stress-Energy Proposal Map

Status of this map: proposal-oriented. Items marked "candidate" are not claims of convergence.

1. **Continuum 1+1 target to cite before writing lattice formulas.** Schottenloher's Axiom 5 gives fields `T_{mu nu}`, symmetry `T_{mu nu}=T_{nu mu}`, and conservation `partial_0 T_{mu0}+partial_1 T_{mu1}=0` for `mu,nu in {0,1}` (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:5046`--`5052`). Luescher-Mack then gives `T_{00}+T_{11}=0` and the chiral combinations `T := T_{00}-iT_{01}` and `bar T := T_{00}+iT_{01}` in that 1+1 CFT setting (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:5054`--`5071`). This is the cleanest local source for the `T_{00}`, `T_{01}`, `T_{11}` dictionary, but it is CFT-specific and not a sourced massive Klein-Gordon stress tensor.

2. **Candidate `T_{00}^{lat}` in 1+1.** Use the chosen local Hamiltonian density `h_j` as the energy density. This follows the report's lattice input contract, where a Hamiltonian comes as `H=sum_j h_j` with local self-adjoint density terms (`report/sections/10_lattice_symmetry_motivation.tex:44`--`65`) and the lattice-fermion source's Hamiltonian-density setup (`references/text/CFTFromLatticeFermions.txt:80`--`100`). For Gaussian chains, the total scalar Hamiltonian is fixed (`report/sections/24_gaussian_boson_symbol_calculus.tex:35`--`40`), but the local split of the `q_x V_r q_{x+r}` term must be fixed as a new lattice convention before this becomes a usable `T_{00}^{lat}`.

3. **Candidate `T_{01}^{lat}` / energy flux in 1+1.** For nearest-neighbour open chains with commuting non-neighbouring densities, define the oriented bond current by the CA-12 commutator density, up to the sign convention fixed with the continuity equation:
   `j^E_{j+1/2} := i[h_j,h_{j+1}]`.
   The checked identity is `i[H, sum_j x_j h_j] = sum_j (x_{j+1}-x_j) i[h_j,h_{j+1}]` (`report/sections/12_lattice_boost_current_1d.tex:66`--`80`; `test/runtests.jl:111`--`115`). Hypothesis for a stress-tensor shard: after choosing orientation, this bond current is the lattice `T_{01}` candidate because continuum energy conservation uses the `mu=0` row of `partial_0 T_{mu0}+partial_1 T_{mu1}=0` (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:5048`--`5052`).

4. **Candidate `T_{10}^{lat}` / momentum density in 1+1.** Do not define this independently at first. The proposal should test whether the integrated energy-current candidate equals the candidate spatial momentum from the Gaussian one-particle construction. The target comparison is between `sum_j j^E_{j+1/2}` from CA-12 and `dGamma(k)` from CA-26 (`report/sections/26_gaussian_boson_generator_algebra.tex:43`--`52`, `:160`--`:170`). Only after that comparison and a symmetric-tensor convention should the report identify `T_{10}^{lat}` with `T_{01}^{lat}`.

5. **Candidate `T_{11}^{lat}` in 1+1.** Define it operationally as the current for the candidate momentum density: find local or quasi-local bond/cell terms `s_{j+1/2}` such that
   `i[H,p_j^{lat}] = s_{j-1/2}-s_{j+1/2}` plus boundary terms.
   This mirrors the Galilean first-moment continuity derivation, where a local continuity equation is model data and does not follow from `H=sum_j h_j` alone (`report/sections/21_lattice_galilean_boosts.tex:52`--`61`, `:63`--`:87`; `src/GalileiAlgebras.jl:101`--`110`). In a 1+1 CFT scaling target, the trace-free source would then become a convergence check against `T_{00}+T_{11}=0` in Schottenloher's convention (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:5054`--`5056`). For massive Gaussian/Klein-Gordon targets, this trace-free check is not sourced here and must not be assumed.

6. **Koo-Saleur relation to the same map.** The sourced Koo-Saleur pattern starts from Fourier modes of a lattice Hamiltonian density (`references/text/CFTFromLatticeFermions.txt:2182`--`2195`) and adds commutator corrections to obtain lattice analogues of `L_k` and `bar L_k` (`references/text/CFTFromLatticeFermions.txt:2197`--`2210`). The source then states convergence of smeared KS approximants in its free massless lattice Dirac setting (`references/text/CFTFromLatticeFermions.txt:2233`--`2235`). Proposal-level interpretation: the first-moment/current calculation is the small-momentum polynomial-weight cousin of the KS Fourier-mode construction, but that analogy is not a theorem in the current Gaussian block.

## Higher-Dimensional Obstacles

- **Components needed.** In spatial dimension `d`, the proposal needs candidates for `T_{00}`, `T_{0a}`, `T_{a0}`, and `T_{ab}` for `a,b=1,...,d`. The current local stress-tensor source is only the 1+1 CFT axiom block (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:5046`--`5056`); Schottenloher does source Poincare generators in arbitrary `D` (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:4092`--`4117`) and the free boson Poincare action (`references/cft/Schottenloher2008/Schottenloher2008.md:4186`--`4244`), but not explicit higher-dimensional local stress-tensor component formulas.
- **Continuity constraints.** A higher-dimensional lattice stress tensor needs oriented face/cell currents satisfying an energy continuity equation for `T_{00}` and momentum-continuity equations for each `T_{a0}`. CA-13 explicitly leaves cell orientation and higher-dimensional convergence unfixed (`report/sections/13_position_dependent_bulk_generators.tex:96`--`100`), and `CONVENTIONS.md:160`--`161` says no higher-dimensional orientation convention is fixed.
- **Rotation constraints.** CA-13 says rotations should be built from momentum-density/current candidates, not from `h_r` alone (`report/sections/13_position_dependent_bulk_generators.tex:73`--`87`). Therefore `T_{a0}^{lat}` must exist before `J_{ab}^{lat}` can be more than a formal moment.
- **Boost constraints.** CA-13's higher-dimensional boost candidate `K_a^{lat}=sum_r r^a h_r` gives a current candidate through `i[H,K_a^{lat}]` (`report/sections/13_position_dependent_bulk_generators.tex:63`--`71`). CA-26 gives the one-particle residuals that this must match: boost-time residual `1/2 partial_a omega^2-k_a` and boost-boost residuals (`report/sections/26_gaussian_boson_generator_algebra.tex:95`--`152`). A local stress-tensor shard must show the real-space current version and the symbol residual version are the same test.
- **Rotation/boost counterexamples.** Hypercubic nearest-neighbour KG is only isotropic to low order near `k=0`, not globally radial (`report/sections/24_gaussian_boson_symbol_calculus.tex:143`--`158`). CA-27's anisotropic and doubler examples show why a correct Hessian is necessary but not sufficient (`report/sections/27_gaussian_boson_residual_conditions.tex:98`--`131`). The stress-tensor proposal must carry the same patch/species data (`report/sections/27_gaussian_boson_residual_conditions.tex:133`--`151`).
- **Boundary constraints.** First moments are cleanest on open lattices, while the Gaussian numerical suite is periodic by default (`CONVENTIONS.md:194`--`199`; `report/sections/28_gaussian_boson_numerical_suite.tex:37`--`59`). A higher-dimensional stress-current check needs either open boxes with boundary terms or a sourced periodic/sawtooth/Fourier construction.
