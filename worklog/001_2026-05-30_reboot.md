Context: the next target stayed in the many-particle Hilbert-space pre-work.
User framing: introduce symmetries as unitary/projective-unitary group

``Hilbert-space compiler'' or ``regular expressions for QM'' — grammar
expressions compile to Hilbert carriers plus observable-algebra data.

### What changed
- Added four report shards:
  - **CA-04** (`04_hilbert_space_compiler_contract.tex`): compiler input/output
    record; carrier, particle grading, observable algebra, symmetry, sector, and
    evidence slots; observable-algebra policy is explicit, never inferred.
  - **CA-05** (`05_symmetry_decorated_grammar.tex`): decorated atoms `(K,U)`;
    ordinary symmetries as unitary reps; projective symmetries via named central
    extensions; propagation through OR, AND, and full Fock.

    quotient triangle; finite-group averaging projection; invariant quotient
    `H/span{U(g)v-v}`; separate invariant vs compressed observable algebras.
  - **CA-07** (`07_exchange_statistics_layer.tex`): exchange symmetry as
    `S_n` sector selection; Bose/Fermi projectors inside full/distinguishable
    Fock; internal symmetries commute with ordinary slot permutations; para and
    genuinely anyonic braid statistics deferred.
- Fixed CONVENTIONS.md:
  - (f) symmetry data in the grammar: unitary reps, projective reps by central
    extension, trivial vacuum action, propagation through grammar.
  - (g) sector/projection/quotient semantics and explicit observable policies.
- Added Julia helpers and checked invariants:
  - `finite_group_average_projector(unitaries)` and
    `is_orthogonal_projection(P)` in `src/CftAnyons.jl`.
  - `test/runtests.jl` checks the `C2` swap action on `C^2`, verifying the
    average `(I+S)/2` is the orthogonal projection onto the invariant line.
- Updated `report.tex`, report maps/catalogue, `INDEX.md`, `Project.toml`,
  `Manifest.toml`, and rebuilt `report.pdf`.

### Why these choices
- The compiler framing follows AQM's QSA discipline: syntax is not a canonical
  quantization claim; every workflow must name inputs, choices, outputs, and
  evidence status.
- Projective representations are routed through central extensions because
  Schottenloher explicitly warns that same-group unitary lifts need not exist in
  general.  This avoids unsourced cocycle arithmetic in the first shard.
- Projections are the primary sector representation because they pin the Hilbert
  structure and support executable checks.  Quotients are allowed only after the
  relation subspace is named.
- Observable algebras are policy choices: `B(H)`, block-preserving, tensor-local,
  invariant, and compressed algebras are intentionally not conflated.

### Frictions / dead ends
- Subagent source mining flagged overclaims to avoid: not every projective rep
  lifts to the same group; not every one-particle unitary implements on a chosen
  CAR/CCR field algebra; general functorial propagation is not a single sourced
  theorem here.  The shards were worded around those limits.
- Initial Julia RED failed before implementation, as intended, and also showed
  `LinearAlgebra` had to be declared/resolved in the project manifest.
- The LaTeX Lamport skeletons used `\small` inside display math; fixed this in
  CA-02--CA-07 while touching the sequence.
